# Audi Q7

This repository contains signal set configurations for the Audi Q7, organized by model year and version. The files are structured to allow for easy differentiation between model generations and other vehicle parameters, ensuring accurate signal mapping for each version of the Audi Q7.

## Generations

The Audi Q7 marked Audi's entry into the luxury SUV segment as their first SUV, evolving through two generations since 2005 to become a flagship luxury seven-seater:

- **First Generation (2006-2016)**: Audi's groundbreaking entry into the luxury SUV market, establishing the brand's SUV credentials:
  - September 2005: Frankfurt Motor Show debut
  - 2006: Production launch at Volkswagen Bratislava Plant, Slovakia
  - First SUV in Audi's history, targeting luxury family market
  - Seven-seat configuration with up to 28 seating arrangements possible
  - Built on Volkswagen Group PL71 platform shared with VW Touareg and Porsche Cayenne
  - World's first series production passenger car V12 TDI diesel engine
  - Premium positioning above mid-size luxury SUV competitors

  **Engine Options:**
  - 3.6L V6 FSI gasoline engine
  - 4.2L V8 FSI gasoline engine
  - 3.0L V6 TDI diesel engine
  - 4.2L V8 TDI diesel engine
  - 6.0L V12 TDI diesel engine (flagship performance option)

  **2009 Facelift:**
  - Redesigned front fascia with elongated air intakes
  - Relocated turn indicators integrated with headlights
  - Uni-colored bumpers for cleaner appearance
  - Transmission upgrade from six-speed to eight-speed automatic

- **Second Generation (2015-present)**: Complete redesign emphasizing weight reduction, technology integration, and enhanced performance:
  - 2015: Launch with significant weight reduction and advanced technology
  - Enhanced aluminum space frame construction
  - Improved fuel efficiency while maintaining luxury positioning
  - Advanced infotainment and connectivity systems
  - Maintained seven-seat practicality with enhanced interior refinement

  **2017-2019 Engine Lineup:**
  - Base: 2.0L turbocharged four-cylinder (252 hp)
  - Premium: 3.0L supercharged V6 (333 hp)
  - Eight-speed automatic transmission standard
  - Elimination of V8 engine options for improved efficiency

  **2019 Mid-Cycle Refresh:**
  - Introduction of signature octagon front grille design
  - Mild-hybrid technology integration across engine lineup
  - Belt-driven starter-generator system for improved efficiency
  - 0.7L/100km fuel consumption reduction through hybridization
  - Enhanced LED lighting technology

  **2020-2024 Continued Evolution:**
  - 2020: Turbocharged 3.0L V6 replacing supercharged unit (335 hp)
  - 2.0L four-cylinder retuned: 248 hp initially, increased to 261 hp
  - Consistent technology and safety system improvements
  - Maintained market position as premium seven-seat SUV

  **2025 Second Facelift:**
  - January 2024: Announcement of comprehensive styling update
  - Enhanced front and rear headlight design evolution
  - HD Matrix LED headlights with Laser high-beam technology
  - Next-generation digital OLED rear lighting signatures
  - New wheel designs and refined exterior details

- **High-Performance SQ7 Variant (2016-present)**: Performance-focused derivative showcasing Audi's engineering capabilities:
  - 2016: Launch with world's most powerful diesel engine at the time
  - 4.0L twin-turbo V8 gasoline engine (507 PS/500 hp)
  - Performance capable of outpacing hot-hatch competitors
  - Seven-seat practicality combined with sports car acceleration
  - Advanced quattro all-wheel drive system
  - Sport-tuned suspension and performance braking systems

- **Future Development**: Third generation in advanced development with anticipated technological advancement:
  - Early 2026: Expected debut alongside new SQ7 variant
  - Enhanced electrification likely with plug-in hybrid options
  - Advanced autonomous driving capabilities anticipated
  - Continued emphasis on luxury, technology, and environmental responsibility
